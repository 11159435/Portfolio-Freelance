Unit 1

Unit 1 – The Online World

This unit provides an in-depth exploration of the internet, covering its history, infrastructure, and the way it has transformed society. We learned about the origins of the internet and how it evolved from a simple network of computers into the complex global system we rely on today. The curriculum covered how data is transmitted across the world using networks, the role of ISPs, and the differences between the World Wide Web and the internet itself.

We examined the structure of web addresses (URLs), domain names, and how DNS servers work to help us navigate online spaces. There was a strong focus on how to use browsers safely and efficiently, including an introduction to search engines, web protocols, and ways to evaluate the credibility of websites. We also discussed the importance of online privacy, digital footprints, and the basics of internet safety and security, such as using strong passwords and recognizing phishing attempts.

Practical skills included setting up email accounts, understanding cloud services, and collaborating online using modern tools. We explored social networks, online communication platforms, and how digital media is shared and accessed. The unit also covered the impact of the internet on daily life—such as e-commerce, online learning, entertainment, and remote work—and highlighted current trends like smart devices and the Internet of Things (IoT).

Skills gained: In this unit, I developed a strong understanding of how the internet functions, how to stay safe online, and how to critically assess information and resources. I improved my ability to use a wide range of online services and tools, learned to protect my digital identity, and gained confidence in navigating the digital world independently and responsibly.
