Unit 2 â€“ Technology Systems

This unit delves into the fascinating inner workings of modern computers and technology systems. We began by studying the essential components that make up a personal computer, such as the central processing unit (CPU), memory (RAM and ROM), storage devices (hard drives, SSDs), and input/output devices. We learned how these parts interact through the motherboard and how data flows within a system using buses and interfaces.

The curriculum covered the boot-up process, the role of the BIOS/UEFI, and the steps required to load an operating system. We investigated how peripheral devices like keyboards, mice, printers, and monitors communicate with the main system, and how drivers enable this communication. Attention was given to different forms of data storage, including magnetic, optical, and solid-state media, and their advantages and disadvantages.

We explored how data is represented in binary form, how it is stored, retrieved, and transferred between components, and the significance of file systems. The unit included practical exercises in assembling and disassembling computers, identifying faulty hardware, upgrading components, and understanding compatibility issues. We also discussed the importance of system maintenance, security, and troubleshooting common hardware problems.

The lessons extended to the structure and function of networks, explaining how computers connect and share resources in both wired and wireless environments. We touched on the basics of network protocols, IP addressing, and the roles of hubs, switches, and routers within a network. Special emphasis was placed on safety procedures, static electricity precautions, and environmental considerations when handling hardware.

Skills gained: Through this unit, I developed a solid understanding of how computers work at a hardware level, how the different parts interact, and how to maintain and troubleshoot systems. I gained confidence in assembling and upgrading PCs, diagnosing hardware problems, and understanding the fundamentals of networking. These skills are essential for anyone who wants to pursue a career in IT or simply become more self-sufficient with modern technology.
