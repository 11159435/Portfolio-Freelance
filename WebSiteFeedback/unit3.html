Unit 3 â€“ Digital Portfolio

This unit is dedicated to the process of planning, designing, and building a digital portfolio from the ground up. We began by exploring what a digital portfolio is and why it is a valuable tool for showcasing skills, achievements, and personal growth in both academic and professional settings.

The curriculum guided us through the stages of portfolio development, starting with setting clear objectives and identifying the audience for our portfolios. We learned how to collect and organize evidence of our work, such as projects, certificates, and creative outputs, and how to select the most relevant pieces to present.

Special emphasis was placed on the principles of effective design and user experience. We studied layout, color theory, typography, and how to structure content for clarity and visual appeal. Lessons included the basics of web technologies, such as HTML and CSS, and how to use digital tools and platforms to build and maintain our portfolios. We also explored the process of reflecting on our progress, setting personal goals, and documenting projects from initial idea to final presentation.

A major component of this unit was the creation of a personal project, which provided practical experience in managing a project lifecycle: planning, executing, revising, and presenting work to an audience. We learned how to give and receive constructive feedback, and how to update our portfolios to reflect ongoing growth and learning.

Skills gained: Through this unit, I developed strong skills in digital organization, content curation, and visual design. I am now able to create and maintain a professional online portfolio, use web technologies to present my work, reflect on my achievements, and communicate my abilities effectively to others.
